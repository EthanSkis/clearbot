CLEARBOT PITCH DECK — BRIEF FOR CLAUDE DESIGN
==============================================

Audience: seed/Series A investors and prospective design-partner customers
   (multi-location restaurant / hospitality / cannabis operators).
Length: 12 slides.
Tone: matter-of-fact, technical, not over-polished. Mirror the product's
   monospace/serif visual language — no stock photos, no AI gradients.
   Black text on white, single accent color (deep teal or muted green),
   one display serif for headings, one geometric sans for body, one mono
   for data labels. No emoji. No drop shadows.

Brand language to reuse verbatim where it fits: "intake → prep → review →
submit → confirm → done"; "audit pack"; "agency engine"; "filings in
flight"; "agency change feed"; "deadline lead time".

A note on honesty: ClearBot today is a real renewal-tracking, packet-
preparing, and reminder-sending product. Actually filing into agency
portals is *scaffolded* (adapter interface, credentials vault) but not
implemented per agency yet — that's the next 12–18 months of work. Don't
imply otherwise. Investors will discount the deck more if the founder
oversells than if scope is honest.

Title: A 50-location operator runs ~400 license renewals a year.

SLIDE 3 — THE SOLUTION
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: ClearBot is the renewal stack itself, not a calendar.
Body — 4 capability bullets (one line each, mono labels):
   ALERT      — emails the right person before each deadline, escalates
                if the filing stalls.
   PREP       — fills the agency's PDF from your license + location data
                and uploads it for review.
   FILE       — submits the packet to the agency portal and pays the fee
                via virtual card (in pilot).
   WATCH      — diffs every agency form/portal daily; bumps any in-flight
                filing back to review when something changes.
Visual: a 4-stage horizontal pipeline. Each stage = a labeled box with
   a one-word state below. Connect with → arrows. Same shape repeats on

SLIDE 4 — HOW IT WORKS (PRODUCT WALKTHROUGH)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: One filing, end to end.
Body: number the steps 1–6, two-line each:
   1. Operator adds a license. ClearBot stores expiry, fee, agency,
      and automation mode (alert / prep / auto).
   2. Daily sweep finds licenses entering the lead window. Opens an
      intake filing and emails the responsible member.
   3. Worker fills the agency's PDF template (or synthesizes one) and
      attaches it to the filing as the packet.
   4. Reviewer opens the filing, opens the packet, hits Approve. Filing
      moves to review → submit.
   5. Adapter logs into the agency portal, uploads the packet, pays the
      fee, captures the confirmation number and receipt.
   6. License expiry rolls forward by its cycle. Activity, packet, and
      receipt land in the audit pack ZIP.
Visual: stacked rounded rectangles ("cards"), one per step, with a left
   gutter showing the stage label (intake/prep/review/submit/confirm/done).
